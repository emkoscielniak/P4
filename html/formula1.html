Formula 1

Rules and Regulations

Formula 1 races follow a strict weekend format with practice sessions on Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the Grand Prix on Sunday. Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers (25 for first place down to 1 for tenth), plus an additional point for the fastest lap if the driver finishes in the top 10. Teams field two drivers per season, and there are typically 20-24 races on the calendar. Cars must comply with detailed technical regulations covering dimensions, weight, power unit specifications, and aerodynamics. During the race, drivers must use at least two different tire compounds and can make strategic pit stops for fresh tires and repairs. Penalties can be issued for various infractions, from time penalties to grid position drops for the next race.

Basic Terminology

DRS (Drag Reduction System)- A movable rear wing element that reduces drag and increases top speed, available in designated zones when within one second of the car ahead

Pole Position- The first starting position on the grid, earned by the fastest driver in qualifying

Pit Stop- When a driver enters the pit lane for tire changes, repairs, or adjustments during a race

Undercut- A strategic move where a driver pits earlier than their rival to gain track position with fresher tires

Slipstream- Following closely behind another car to benefit from reduced air resistance and gain speed
