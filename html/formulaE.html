Formula E

Rules and Regulations

Formula E races, called E-Prix, are held on temporary street circuits in major cities around the world. Each race day features practice, qualifying, and the race itself, all condensed into a single day format. Unlike Formula 1, Formula E uses a unique qualifying system with groups and duels to determine the starting grid. The races are energy-limited rather than time-limited, meaning drivers must manage their battery usage strategically throughout the race. Attack Mode is a distinctive feature where drivers can activate extra power by driving through a designated zone off the racing line. Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers, and drivers must complete the race in a single car with careful energy management being crucial for success.

Basic Terminology

Attack Mode- Extra power boost activated by driving through a special activation zone off the racing line

FanBoost- Additional energy given to the top voted drivers by fans before each race

Energy Management- The strategic use of battery power throughout the race to ensure completion

Regeneration- The process of recovering energy during braking to recharge the battery

Halo- Protective device above the cockpit designed to protect the driver's head
